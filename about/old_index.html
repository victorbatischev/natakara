What is PILATES

PILATES comes from the name of a person, who was German (Joseph Pilates).
He had been sick of rickets, asthma and rheumatism.  He had to play in the home and had envied the people who enjoyed playing outside.  He realized the healthy body brought happiness.  After then, he tried many kinds of physical training, western body building, oriental and old Greek method.

And Mr. Pilates thought he should teach his method to the other people who were injured at the World War 1 and who could not leave from the bed.  He developed the new training machine for the people who could enjoy the exercise on the bed.  Owing to that machine, death rate was decreasing and the highly motivated people for the health were increasing.

The good reputation was spreading to the US.  Finally, he settle the studio in New York and not only injured people but also Dancer, Housewives, Actress and many kinds of people learned about the PILATES there.
He developed more than 600 exercise and approached to the total care about the body, mind and spiritual things.

His goal was all the people should be able to obtain the well grown body and mind through the exercise.

The difference between PILATES and Yoga

It’s often said PILATES and Yoga are almost same.  But they are clearly different.  Yoga focuses on the stretch of the body.  On the other hand, PILATES improve the inner muscle at the same time, stretching.  And PILATES adjusts the frame of the bones and builds an effective body.
 
The way of breathing is also different. Yoga takes the abdominal breathing.  But PILATES activates sympathetic nerve and refreshes the brain by taking the thoracic breathing.  It would help us keeping well autonomic nerve.
 
Especially, PILATES improve the inner muscle recognizing each muscle surrounding the bone consciously.

In general weight training, it makes strong muscle more rigid and more shorten.  But PILATES makes the muscle lengthen and flexible.  Many Athletes and Dancers are getting interested in PILATES these days.
